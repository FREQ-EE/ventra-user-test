Ventra Field Test: Instructions & Feedback

Please use Ventra naturally in your usual music workflow. When you have formed an opinion (or within 7 days), complete the survey below. If you discover issues later, you may submit again.

What we are testing

  • Plug-and-play experience (no software installation)
  • Breath control feel: smoothness, usable control range, consistency
  • Reliability: reconnects, rest-state silence, dropouts
  • Musical usefulness in real contexts (your patches, your playing, your setup)

This is not a structured lab session. We mainly want your real-world impressions.

Quick start

  1. Plug Ventra into your computer via USB.
  2. Open your DAW. Ventra will be registered automatically as a MIDI input device.
  3. Attach either mouthpiece and play:
    • Closed mouthpiece: no airflow (pressure builds up).
    • Flow mouthpiece: vented airflow (air escapes).
    The mouthpieces are interchangeable. If possible, please test both across your sessions.
  4. Load an instrument or synth plugin. Either use a patch that already responds to breath/expression, or use your DAW/plugin’s MIDI learn to map a parameter to Ventra’s breath control (CC2).
  5. Play and confirm you have a smooth, usable control range from minimum to maximum breath.

Technical: Ventra outputs breath pressure as MIDI Control Change CC2 (Breath Controller). Map CC2 to any parameter (expression, volume, filter cutoff, etc.) as required.

Suggested usage (optional, but helpful)

  • Use Ventra across at least 2 sessions on different days if possible.
  • Please test both mouthpieces (Closed and Flow) if you can, and note which you prefer in the survey.
  • Try one context with long phrases (pads/strings/winds) and one with repeated attacks.
  • If practical, record a short clip (30–60 s) and keep it for reference.

If something goes wrong, describe what you were doing (DAW, patch, routing) and whether reconnecting fixed it.

Troubleshooting (common)

  • Ventra not visible as MIDI input: unplug/replug; try a different USB port; restart the DAW.
  • No response in the instrument: confirm the track is armed/monitoring; confirm the plugin is receiving MIDI; confirm the patch/parameter is mapped (via MIDI learn) to CC2; check your DAW is not filtering CC2.
  • It feels too sensitive / not sensitive enough: please describe this in the survey (we will adjust defaults).
  • Stuck values / not returning to silence: note the conditions and whether reconnecting resolves it.
